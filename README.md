# FUTURE_CS_02
Email Phishing Awareness

Overview

This repository contains a Phishing Detection & Awareness Analysis focused on identifying and understanding common phishing email attacks.

The purpose of this project was to analyze phishing email examples, identify indicators of compromise, classify risk levels, and provide clear prevention guidelines that non-technical users can easily understand.


Project Objectives
Analyze phishing email samples
Identify phishing indicators
Classify emails as Safe / Suspicious / Phishing
Explain phishing techniques in simple, business-friendly language
Provide clear prevention and awareness guidelines
Present findings in a professional security report

This project focuses on risk identification and user awareness, not exploitation.

Tools Used

Browser Developer Tools	- Inspect email elements and sender information
Email Header Analyzers -	Analyze SPF/DKIM and routing details

Findings Summary
1. Spoofed Sender Domains

Observation:
Some emails used sender addresses that did not match the legitimate company domain.

Risk Level: Medium
Business Impact: Users may trust fraudulent emails and disclose sensitive information.

2. Social Engineering & Fear Tactics

Observation:
Emails used urgent or threatening language to pressure recipients into immediate action.

Risk Level: High
Business Impact: Panic-driven decisions increase the likelihood of credential theft and malware infections.

3. Suspicious Links and Hidden Redirects

Observation:
Buttons and hyperlinks redirected users to domains unrelated to the legitimate organization.

Risk Level: High
Business Impact: Users may be redirected to credential harvesting or malware distribution sites.

4. Generic Greetings and Formatting Tricks

Observation:
Emails used generic greetings and visual formatting to imitate legitimate corporate communication.

Risk Level: Medium
Business Impact: Users may fail to recognize fraudulent intent due to professional appearance.
